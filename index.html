My Story

My Resume(for a quick brief)

When I was a child, I loved video games and had a passion for technology. I always wanted to be the person to solve complex problems and especially ones related to technology. I knew I liked problem solving from a young age, and spent much of my time researching that in grade-school. I finished calculus 2 by junior year and was studying Advanced modern Physics in my junior year.

I went to CMU for a few semesters on a scholarship, but I decided to make a big leap and move out of my home-town. I went to UCCS instead and studied Physics and Mathematics, but I also took classes in C# and C++ while I was there. I also took game design as an elective. I chose to major in physics because I loved problem solving, and I exceeded in both math and physics. Both of those subjects just happen to be the bread and butter of software!

While studying, I built several applications for an escape room I was working at. These projects ranged from creating automated puzzles with arduinos and raspberry pi's, to web development, and to general API call's, to keep track of puzzle progress from the back-end. From then on, I knew I wanted to develop software for my career because of how fullfilling it was to spend my time doing that.

Now, I am currently working as a Level 2 Software Support Engineer in Microsoft's Enterprise Security & Compliance team. I continue to develop software as a free-lance agent, working currently for a few record labels that are looking to improve their website's functionality.

To maintain my skills, I also practice on websites like HackerRank, and I enjoy partaking in Capture The Flag activities online.
